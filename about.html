Profile

I use research, structure, and visual craft to make complex things easier to understand.

I came into HCI through architecture and image-making, so I do not treat structure and visuals as separate things. Architecture trained me to think through hierarchy, constraints, documentation, and how people move through systems. Illustration trained my eye for mood, composition, detail, and visual identity.

That combination helps me work across the messy middle of a project: understanding the problem, organizing information, shaping the flow, and making the final experience feel intentional. I am building toward UX, interaction design, and research roles where clarity and craft both matter.

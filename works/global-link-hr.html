Website Design

Global Link HR Website

A self-initiated, unpaid website build for Global Link HR, created to learn how a real service website comes together across site architecture, identity, multilingual support, responsive layout, contact paths, and offline brand touchpoints.

View Website

Role

Website structure, UI direction, Wix build, QR card design

Project Type

Independent website build

Platform

Wix site builder, multilingual tools, domain setup

Timeline

May 2025 to August 2025

Context

I used this project to learn how a business website works beyond the visible layout.

I built this as a self-initiated learning project for a business contact. I was not treating it as paid client work. The point was to reduce pressure, learn from a real site build, and understand the practical layers behind a service website.

The site needed to introduce an HR and recruitment business, explain services to employers and job seekers, support international communication, and create a more credible online presence.

Identity System

The visual direction was built around a simple corporate identity system.

The master plan set a clear base: deep corporate blue, silver, white, and light gray. I used that direction to keep the site professional, clean, and easy to scan.

Site Architecture

I planned the site around the two main audiences: employers and job seekers.

01

Homepage

Hero message, contact action, quick introduction, services overview, testimonials, and footer links.

02

About and services

Mission, leadership context, company values, employer solutions, job seeker support, and global reach.

03

Employer and job seeker paths

Separate entry points for talent acquisition, resume submission, inquiries, resources, and future listings.

04

Multilingual access

Language support helped the site fit a cross-cultural recruitment audience instead of staying locked to one region.

05

Trust sections

Testimonials, FAQ content, contact information, policy links, and business details supported credibility.

06

Contact flow

Forms, contact details, map/address content, and clear navigation helped visitors move toward next steps.

Website Features

The build became a small system of pages, features, and brand touchpoints.

The Wix site included a hero message around connecting talent with opportunities globally, a recruitment-focused navigation, testimonial content, FAQ sections, contact information, language support, and footer/legal links.

I also designed a business card with a QR code so the offline identity could connect back to the website. That made the site part of a larger handoff, not just a standalone page.

Audience

Employers and job seekers

The site had to support both sides of recruitment without making the homepage feel overloaded.

Access

Translation and QR flow

Language support and QR scanning helped connect international users and offline contacts to the site.

Platform

Wix constraints

Working inside Wix taught me how design decisions change when layout, forms, language tools, and hosting live inside one platform.

What I Worked On

I handled the project from planning and page structure to launch support.

01

Master plan

Outlined site pages, audience flows, content needs, color direction, platform choice, and build timeline.

02

Wix page build

Built the site sections, adjusted layout behavior, and worked through platform-specific design constraints.

03

Service organization

Structured employer services, job seeker support, industry content, FAQs, and contact pathways.

04

Responsive layout

Adjusted sections so the site could hold up across desktop and smaller screens.

05

Language support

Explored multilingual setup so the site could better fit an international recruitment audience.

06

QR business card

Designed the business card and QR handoff so physical networking could lead directly to the website.

Learning Outcome

This project taught me that a real website needs structure, trust, and handoff, not just layout.

01

No-code tools still need design judgment

Wix made the build faster, but I still had to make decisions about hierarchy, spacing, content, navigation, and consistency.

02

Business sites need clear audience paths

An HR website has to speak to employers and job seekers without losing clarity on the first screen.

03

Launch work includes invisible details

Domain setup, platform limits, forms, multilingual support, and footer/legal content shaped the final experience.

04

Offline and online touchpoints should connect

The QR business card helped connect a physical introduction to the website and made the brand feel more complete.

Reflection

Global Link HR helped me move from page design into website systems thinking.

This was one of my first full website builds around a real business identity. It helped me understand how content, structure, platform choices, responsive behavior, contact paths, and brand handoff all affect whether a website feels credible.

If I revisited it now, I would refine the UX writing, tighten the conversion paths, plan analytics earlier, and make the visual system more consistent across every page.

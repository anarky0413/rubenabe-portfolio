Clearing the Queue:

A Help Desk Redesign

A user-centered redesign for managing student support requests at the DePaul Library help desk. The project focused on reducing staff stress, improving task clarity, and making daily support work easier to manage.

View Presentation See Research Process

Smart Task Queue

Help desk staff need faster ways to track, prioritize, and resolve student requests.

High Priority Room Booking Tech Checkout Research Help

Role

UX research, interface design, usability testing

Project Type

Help desk workflow redesign

Methods

Interviews, contextual inquiry, personas, testing

Focus

Task clarity, prioritization, staff support

Overview

Helping library staff manage support work with less friction.

DePaul library help desk staff handle overlapping student requests, technical questions, room bookings, and walk-in support. Without a clear system to track or prioritize requests, staff can lose context, repeat work, or feel overloaded during busy periods.

The redesign creates a lightweight internal tool for monitoring active tasks, checking quick help resources, viewing student request history, and adjusting dashboard information around each staff member’s workflow.

Research

We studied the daily workflow of DePaul library help desk staff.

What we did

Interviewed 9 library staff

We gathered feedback from Access and Research Help desk staff to understand day-to-day support needs, pain points, and workflow breakdowns.

What we noticed

Memory carried too much of the workflow

Staff relied on sticky notes, verbal updates, and personal memory because there was no centralized way to manage multiple active requests.

Why it mattered

Task overlap created stress

Overlapping responsibilities caused confusion, delays, and extra pressure, especially for new staff still learning the desk workflow.

Problem Space

The help desk needed a clearer support flow.

01

Staff juggle tech issues, room bookings, online chats, reference questions, and walk-in requests.

02

There was no clear way to track, prioritize, or hand off multiple student requests.

03

New staff needed fast access to routine help steps without depending on supervisors for every small task.

Mid-Level Staff

New Hire

Design Direction

The redesign focused on four support features.

01

Smart Task Queue

Prioritizes student requests by urgency, wait time, and request type.

02

Quick Help Panel

Gives new staff searchable support steps for common library tasks.

03

Student Request History

Shows past issues so staff can provide faster and more personalized support.

04

Customizable Dashboard

Lets staff adjust layout and notifications around their daily workflow.

Interface Concept

A dashboard that makes task status visible at a glance.

The proposed system centers on an active queue where staff can see request type, wait time, urgency, student context, and task status without switching between scattered notes or verbal updates.

The main interaction goal was to support quick decision-making during busy periods while still giving new staff guidance through the Quick Help panel.

Scenarios

We tested the concept through staff-centered task scenarios.

Alex

Jamie searches the Quick Help panel for “Tech Checkout” and follows the steps without interrupting another staff member.

Usability Testing

Testing showed where the interface needed to become simpler.

What we tested

  • Can users complete tasks without confusion?
  • Do users understand the task queue and help panel?
  • Are feedback and error messages clear?

Participant reactions

“Can’t get easier than this.”
“I had to guess too much. During hectic times, I’d want things simpler.”
“Color coding is very helpful in identifying.”

Design Updates

The final updates focused on clearer labels, less clutter, and better guidance.

Button Labels

Make actions more direct

Reword vague labels like “Take Next” into clearer options such as “Start Task.”

Queue View

Reduce visual overload

Simplify the task queue by grouping requests and reducing visible controls upfront.

Micro-Guidance

Support new staff faster

Explore expandable help tools or chatbot-style guidance for routine questions.

Feedback

Show clearer status cues

Add confirmations and next-step guidance so staff know when a task has moved forward.

Outcome

Clearer task flow, faster support access, and less reliance on memory.

The redesign clarified how help desk staff could track active requests, prioritize urgent student needs, and access quick support steps without relying on sticky notes, verbal updates, or memory.

01 Clearer task priority
02 Faster access to routine help
03 Less reliance on verbal handoffs
04 Better support for new staff

Reflection

Designing for staff meant designing for pressure, not just tasks.

This project showed how small workflow gaps can create real stress in service environments. The design was not just about adding a dashboard, but about making priorities, next steps, and support resources easier to understand during busy desk conditions.

My main contribution was connecting the research findings to workflow features, including the task queue, quick help panel, student request history, and usability testing updates.

If I continued this project, I would refine the interface with a stronger visual system, test the revised task labels again, and explore how the quick help feature could support onboarding for new staff.
